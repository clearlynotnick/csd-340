Ed continued to look away while Ellis spoke. Once he was done speaking, a moment passed before Ed slowly looked over at Ellis. "How are you doing Ellis?" Without looking away and without missing a beat, Ellis spoke. "You're looking at it. I gotta say...you're looking older." Ed kept a solemn scowl standing upright, resolute, but seemingly stressed. "I am older," Ed spoke. Ellis continued to look at Ed firmly with conviction.

"Got a _________ from your wife," Ellis began again without hesitating. "She writes me pretty regular...keepin' me up on the family news...told me you're quittin'?" Ellis' anger began spiking at the end of the sentence, seemingly seething with every last word. Ed breathed out slowly, taking a moment before removing his Sheriff's hat. "You want a cup," Ed finally broke the silence, walking towards the already made _________ pot. "'Preciate it," retorted Ellis. Silence held in the air as as Ed trudged over and began pouring the coffee, before stopping and examining the pot. "How free is that coffee?" Ellis rolled his wheelchair towards Ed slowly. "I generally make a fresh pot every week...even if there's some leftover."
